Welcome to the TRICLE calculator


Tricle calculator helps you to calculate radii of various circles related to a triangle namely:-

Incircle: The largest circle that will fit and just touch each side of the triangle. Its center (incenter) is the intersection of all three angle bisectors of a triangle.

Circumcircle: The circle that passes through all three vertices of a triangle. Its center (circumcenter) is the point at which the perpendicular bisectors of the sides of a triangle intersect.

Orthocircle: The point of intersection of the three altitudes of a triangle is called as orthocenter. The circle with orthocenter as its center, that passes through the vertices of a new triangle formed by drawing sides parallel to the original triangle is called as the orthocircle of that original triangle

Nine-point circle: The circle that passes through nine points - midpoints of each side, foot of each altitude and midpoint of the line segment from each vertex of the triangle to the orthocenter. Its center (nine-point center) is the midpoint of the circumcenter and the orthocenter of the triangle.

Excircle: the triangle is a circle lying outside the triangle, tangent to one of its sides and tangent to the extensions of the other two.
